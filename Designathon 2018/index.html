MASS Collective Laser Cutter

About the laser

The laser at Mass collective is a 150 Watt, 36 in x 24 in bed, with a 12 in depth adjustment. It has the capacity to cut up to an inch thick of appropriate material (see materials list). It has the abilty to engrave a wide variety of materials such as glass, although it cannot cut through it. We offer onsite graphical processing, 2D and 3D design work, and for quantites more than 1,000 units, we can set up rotary engraving on cylinders, glasses, spheres. We can also make high-quiality, reusable stencils as well as permanent marking on metal such as steel, copper, aluminum, and brass. We have a training program for apprentices who want to learn how to operate the machine.

Materials

What materials are compatible with the laser


    Compatible Materials
  • Acrylic/Lucite/Plexiglas/PMMA
  • Aluminum
    • Harms laser if improperly guarded
  • Aspen
  • Balsa
  • Baltic Birch
  • Basewood
  • Brass
    • Not suggested if inexperienced>
  • Carbon Fiber
    • cannot be coated with anything
  • Cardboard
    • Watch for fire
  • Ceramic
    • must cut slowly
  • Cloth/felt
    • Cannot be plastic coated or impregnated
  • Cork
    • Avoid thicker cork
  • Cloroplast
  • Delrin (POM)
  • Depron foam
    • Monitor closely
  • Gator foam
    • Difficult to cut, monitor closely
  • Kapton tape (Polyimide)
  • Leather/suede
    • Must be real leather, no pleather or faux leather
  • Magnetic Sheet
  • Wood
    • Avoid oily woods or resinous woods as they may catch fire
  • MDF engineered woods
  • Mylar
    • gold-coated Mylar will not work
  • Nylon
  • Cardstock
  • Plywood
If the material you want to use is not on this list, look for another material that fits the criteria.
